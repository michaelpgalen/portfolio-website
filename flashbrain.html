PHASE 1: Discovery

Competitor Analysis

I began my discovery with market research. I identified three competitors from the Apple app store to analyze: Quizlet, AnkiApp, and Flash Cards. Analysis included usability, experience, and success in meeting user goals.

Summary of Findings

  • Two of the three apps were successful in meeting the user goal: Quizlet & AnkiApp
  • While Quizlet felt a bit cluttered at times, reducing the ease of navigation, unique extra features made up for it in added value to the user (variety of activities, community engagement, etc)
  • AnkiApp is designed very well to meet the user goal of vocabulary learning. It feels uninspired though. It doesn’t seem like the designers thought about how to go beyond the basic functions to add more value to our user.
  • Flash Cards failed to meet the user goal, and as a result, would likely be deleted quickly.

User Research - Interviews

Recruiting

I recruited four participants from my personal network via personal calls and confirmation messages. Participant requirements:

  • Have used flashcards to learn vocabulary
  • Recently tried to learn new vocabulary for some purpose
  • Use or have used a learning app

Organizing the Data

All interviews were audio recorded and analyzed, and then key insights were consolidated into three categories: Thinking, Feeling, and Doing.

Analysis

Themes were identified and ranked by their prevalence.

Proto-Persona Creation

Combining data from all four interviews, I created a proto-persona named Hugo. Formulating the data into one proto-persona allowed me to focus on and empathize with a single person’s needs, goals, and context while designing solutions. 

Meet Hugo, a brewmaster.

He’s in his 30s, married and has a degree in communications. He loves the beer industry and is similarly getting interested in the wine industry. He frequently uses free learning apps for different subjects related to both work and hobbies.

User Stories

Based on the interview data, user stories were created for Hugo.

As Hugo, I want

  • to remember and assimilate new vocabulary so that I can move into new aspects of my industry and adjacent industries.
  • to be able to access and review new vocabulary quickly and easily so that I can study frequently at sporadic moments.
  • a way to engage multiple senses with new vocabulary I’m learning so that I can create stronger memories and stories for the terms.
  • to see the function and context of new terms so that I understand the value and am more motivated to retain the vocabulary.

Challenges facing Hugo

  • Limited study time
  • Limited opportunities to observe terms in context
  • Some free flashcard apps have been unstable (crashes)
  • Limited opportunity to physicalize his learning or engage multiple senses
  • Difficult to assimilate new terms

Problem & Hypothesis

Having my user data synthesized into a proto-persona with clear needs and goals (user stories), I was able to formulate (a clear problem statement) and a design hypothesis to meet Hugo’s needs.

Problem

Hugo needs a convenient way to retain and assimilate new vocabulary because his industry is always creating new terms for beer/wine flavors and descriptions.

Hypothesis

By creating a vocabulary app with multi-media descriptions of terms, progress feedback, and various modes of learning for Hugo, Hugo will be able to assimilate new terms into industry conversations quicker.

Ready for Phase 2 - Design Strategy and Prototypes!

Phase 2: Strategy & Prototypes

Task Analysis & User Flows

Having a list of user stories for Hugo, allowed me to break down tasks from Hugo’s stories. I broke them down into their smallest steps and decision possibilities, and then created user flows to visualize the architecture and primary interactions.

Lofi Wireframes to Prototypes

User flows provided the skeleton for me to create basic lofi pen and paper wireframes. I start with pen and paper to sketch and iterate ideas quickly. They allow me to focus on the high-level experience, without getting distracted with time-consuming details.

Lofi paper wireframes set the ground for midfi clickable prototypes. I used Adobe XD to turn my lofi wireframes into basic clickable prototypes.

Phase 3: Testing and Reiterating

Usability Tests

Now, with clickable midfi prototypes, it was time to pause on making anymore design decisions, and turn my focus to validating (and invalidating) my designs with usability testing.

Recruiting & Test Plan

I recruited four participants from my personal network to test the usability of my prototype, as well as gather thoughts and feelings on the user experience. Test sessions were scheduled over two days and recorded with video and audio for future reference and analysis. Errors were measured and documented using Jakob Nielsen’s Error Severity Rating Scale.

Results and Re-iterating Designs

The test sessions exposed some clear flaws in the designs via errors in completing the given tasks. Observations and reflections also influenced design revisions, improving the experience and usability of the mid-fidelity prototype.
